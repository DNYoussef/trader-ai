The multiplicative derivative has in some cases technical advantages of calculus,

In this work we pretend to show some possible and explicit applications of the PC in
Economics. More precisely, in section 2, we introduce a relation that appears between the
multiplicative derivative and the known concept of the elasticity. The elasticity concept is
too much extended and useful, a short glossary considers: Cross-price elasticity of demand,
Cross-price elasticity of supply, Elastic (elasticity > 1), Income elasticity of demand,
Inelastic (elasticity < 1), Inferior goods (income elasticity < 0), Luxuries (income elasticity
> 1), Necessity (income elasticity < 1), Normal goods (income elasticity > 0), Perfectly
elastic curves, Perfectly inelastic curves, Price elasticity of demand, Price elasticity of
supply, Price elasticity and Unit elastic (elasticity = 1). We think that the concept of
elasticity deserves to have in property an associate calculus, and the PC is a good candidate.
In section 3, we connect the new derivative with the homogeneous functions, essentially we
presents a new representation for the Euler Theorem. Moreover, we do some special
remarks of two important classes of these functions, we represent the Cobb Douglas
Functions as linear transformations, and for Constant Elasticity of Substitution Functions
(for to calculate the substitution elasticity) we get a formula that is easy to remember.
